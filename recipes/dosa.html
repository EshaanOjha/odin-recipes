Dosa

Description:

A dosa is a popular South Indian, thin, savory crepe or pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and black lentils, similar in appearance to a crepe but with a distinct, slightly tangy flavor and crispy texture when cooked. Served hot, it's often filled with spiced potatoes (Masala Dosa) or enjoyed plain with accompaniments like sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and chutneys (coconut, mint, etc.).

Ingredients:

  • Rice
  • Dal (split black gram)
  • fenugreek seeds
  • Poha
  • Curd/Yogurt

Steps to make a Dosa:

  1. Soak rice and urad dal (with a little fenugreek) for 4–6 hours.
  2. Grind to a smooth batter with water.
  3. Let it ferment overnight (8–12 hours).
  4. Heat a tawa (pan) and lightly grease it.
  5. Pour a ladle of batter and spread thin in a circle.
  6. Drizzle a little oil, cook until golden and crisp.
  7. Fold and serve hot with chutney or sambar.
